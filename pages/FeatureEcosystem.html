Affinity

Generate dynamic attraction rules between scatter-layers

Ecosystem Affinity

If you'd like your scatter-system to be dynamically attracted by other scatter-system(s), that's where the "Affinity" feature comes in handy. Choose your system affinity, a max range & transition distance, then define the influence on scale or density of your wish to create the distribution rule.

Contact Method

By default, the proximity contact will be your scattered point's origin. If you'd like a more precise collision distance field, it is possible to change the collision surface to 'Convex-hull' or use the realized instances meshes, however, this is not recommended as it might be too performance intensive.

Limit Collision Option

A limit collision slider is available to limit spawn distance near your affinity object.

Repulsion

Avoid nearby proximity in-between scatter-layers

Ecosystem Repulsion

If you'd like your scatter-system to be dynamically repulsed by other scatter-system(s), that's where the "Repulsion" feature comes in handy. Choose your repulsive system(s), a max range & transition distance, then define the influence on the scale or density you wish to create such distribution rules.

Contact Method

Just like the affinity feature, by default, the proximity contact will be your scattered point's origin. If you'd like a more precise collision distance field, it is possible to change the collision surface to 'Convex-hull' or use the realized instances meshes, however, this is not recommended as it might be too performance intensive.

Density

Scatter your instances only on dense or sparse areas

Ecosystem Density

If you'd like your scatter-system to be spawn dynamically only in areas where other scatter are dense/scarse, choose the ecosystem density feature. In order to work, you will need to choose up to 3 systems upon where we will analyse their instances per square area density.


Sampled Area

For this feature to work perfectly, you will need to evaluate the correct sample area size upon a number of instances will be counted to count the density value.

Ecosystem & Biomes

Biomes are often using the ecosystem feature!

Ecosystems & Biomes

Like most features, all ecosystem settings can be exported within a .biome format. Make sure that there are no dependencies issues, by selecting every layer and their dependencies when doing the biome export!
